SU2 Dev. Team at University of Brescia

The research activity focuses on the development of innovative computational fluid dynamics (CFD) codes and in the application of CFD to flow simulations of industrial interest. This activity is performed with both in house developed and commercial codes, which are mainly used for the analysis and design of turbomachinery (axial and centrifugal compressors, axial and radial thermal turbines, wind turbines).

Antonio Ghidoni

Assistant Professor at the Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering

My research interests include the development of algorithms for the unstructured mesh generation and of a high-order accurate method (Discontinuous Galerkin method) for computational fluid dynamics (high-order temporal scheme, transition model, hp-multigrid methods, ILES, hybrid RANS-LES approaches). In particular, in cooperation with the TU Delft SU2 team, I am developing the UMG3 code for the automatic generation of anisotropic hybrid 3D meshes and some feature for turbomachinery simulations in SU2.

Marco Lorini

Ph.D. candidate at the Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering

My research activity is mainly focused on the prediction of the laminar-to-turbulent transition for turbomachinery and aerospace applications. I have been implementing both experimental and physics-based transition models in a code based on a high-order accurate Discontinuous Galerkin method for compressible flows. I approached SU2 for an automotive application: the aerodynamic design of the Formula SAE vehicle for the University of Brescia team. My objective is to increase my experience on SU2 and implement new features for turbomachinery applications.

Gianmaria Noventa

Ph.D. candidate at the Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering

My research activity is mainly focused on the development of a high-order accurate Discontinuous Galerkin method for incompressible unsteady turbulent flows to investigate the aerodynamic performance and the design of a vertical axis wind turbine (VAWT). In particular I am working on high-order time integration schemes, preconditioner and hybrid RANS-LES approaches. I approached SU2 for the aerodynamics design of the Formuale SAE vehicle of the University of Brescia team. My objective regarding SU2 is to implement new features for wind turbines simulations and compare with our in-house high-order code in terms of performance and accuracy.
