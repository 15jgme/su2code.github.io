SU2 Dev. Team at the University of Strathclyde

The fluid mechanics group at the University of Strathclyde has extensive experience with the development and application of implicit Large Eddy Simulations, particularly for compressible transitional and turbulent flows, including turbulent boundary layers and turbulent mixing. Furthermore, the group is working on the development and application of multi-scale and molecular dynamics methods.

Prof. Dimitris Drikakis

I am interested in the development and implementation of high-order methods for Implicit Large Eddy Simulations in the framework of SU2. I have nearly 30 years of experience in CFD and fluid mechanics and my current research focuses on compressible turbulent boundary layer flows, Richmyer-Meshkov & Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities and compressible turbulent mixing. Future work in the framework of SU2 may also include the development of multi-scale methods that will enable coupling of SU2 with mesoscale and atomistic models. More information can be found on www.drikakis.com.

Dr. Michael Frank

My research interests in SU2 are to develop accurate and computationally efficient algorithms for ILES. Of particular interest is the use of high-order, Discontinuous Galerkin methods as ILES models and to investigate how these methods compare with their finite volume counterparts. Other future work in the framework of SU2 may also include the investigation of heat transfer properties in micro- and nano-fluidic systems and thermal properties of nanofluids.

Dr. Ioannis William Kokkinakis

My research interests in SU2 are to implement and investigate the accuracy of high-order methods in ILES simulations, including comparisons of high-order finite volume based schemes and Discontinuous Galerkin (DG) methods and the development of high-order methods for RANS, ILES/RANS and DES computational frameworks with applications ranging from near wall flows to turbulent mixing.
