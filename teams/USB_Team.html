SU2 Dev. Team at the University of Basilicata

Our group specializes in the development of Residual Distribution (also known as Fluctuation Splitting) schemes and unstructured shock-fitting techniques. At present, we are not developing SU2, but rather using it to perform code-to-code verification. We are particularly interested in assessing how different unstructured grid codes perform on purely triangular meshes. We might also consider interfacing our unstructured shock-fitting algorithm with SU2.

Raffaele Pepe

PhD candidate in the School of Engineering

Raffaele has just completed his PhD and will soon defend his dissertation titled "“Numerical Simulation Of Multi-Dimensional Hypersonic Plasma Flows”. In his PhD work Raffaele has implemented a chemical model of an Argon plasma within the in-house eulfs code; has extended the unstructured shock-fitting algorithm to make it capable of dealing with flows in chemical and thermo-chemical non-equilibrium, has interfaced the shock-fitting algorithm with the VKI in-house CoolFluid code. Raffaele has also done limited testing of the multi-fluid capability of SU2.


We have tetsted SU2 on three verification cases; a brief report, grids and SU2 configuration files are made available below. A fourth testcase is under construction.

  1. Un-steady convection of an inviscid vortex. Can we get second order spatial and temporal accuracy using truly unstructured triangular grids?
    1. report;
    2. grids, SU2 configuration files and scripts
  2. 2D NACA 0012 Airfoil Validation Case (turbulent flow with SA model). Can we get reasonable results using triangular meshes also in the boundary layer? Roe's first order works, what about second order?
    1. report;
    2. grids; the 2D grids provided by NASA have been cut into triangles (preserving symmetry wrt. the chord);
    3. the SU2 configuration file is the one in TestCasesv3.2.0/rans/naca0012/: change the mesh filename and the boundary labels.
  3. 2D RAE 2822. This triangular mesh has not been derived from a structured one, but features a quasi-structured boundary layer mesh. Roe's first order works, what about second order?
    1. report;
    2. grid and SU2 configuration file.
  4. Compressible point source flow. Is it true that shock-fitting preserves the formal accuracy of the scheme in the shock-downstream region, whereas shock-capturing reduces to first-order?
