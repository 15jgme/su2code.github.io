SU2 Dev. Team at TU Delft

Our group performs research on innovative "green" systems for propulsion and power generation. We develop new knowledge on the fluid dynamics and thermodynamics of dense vapors and supercritical fluids by performing theoretical, numerical and experimental studies, and apply it to the study of innovative technologies like the Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC) turbogenerator or the supercritical CO2 Brayton turbine. Part of our investigations covers also combustion and turbomachinery aspects of advanced gas turbines. We focus in addition on the development of system and turbomachinery fluid dynamic automated design methods.

Matteo Pini

Assistant Professor, Propulsion and Power

I approached the SU2 community with passion and enthusiasm. I have co-authored and supervised the extension of the compressible solver to arbitrarily complex fluid models. My current research interests are devoted to expanding the capability of SU2 to the simulation of challenging turbomachinery flow fields ( we are busy with the implementation of the mixing-plane, and aim at unsteady multi-rows simulations). My final objective is to develop new adjoint-based design methodologies for small-scale turbomachinery operating close to the critical point of fluids. This research will allow to devise high-efficient and highly compact energy systems for heat recovery onboard of aircraft and helicopters using supercritical fluids as working media.

Salvatore Vitale

PhD Candidate, Propulsion and Power

My first contribution to the SU2 software is the generalization of the compressible RANS solver to treat not ideal fluid flows. Specifically, I have been implementing generalized numerical methods for both inviscid and viscous fluxes and a thermo-physical property library . Furthermore, the structure of the compressible solver has been generalized to use different thermo-physical models. My research is the design of highly efficient small multi-stage unconventional turbines dealing with dense vapours and supercritical fluids. In the future I am going to be working on the implementation of features to treat not ideal compressible flows in turbomachinery (i.e Mixing-plane, Giles BC, sliding mesh) both at direct and adjoint solver level.
