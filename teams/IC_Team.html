SU2 Dev. Team at Imperial College London

Our group is focused on increasing the efficiency of the next generation of air vehicles and wind turbines by exploiting aeroelastic phenomena and effectively understanding the mechanisms of aerodynamic actuation, which will allow effective control of flexible lightweight wings and blades.

Rafael Palacios

Reader in Aeronautics, Department of Aeronautics, Imperial College London

My main focus is on aeroelastic modelling of highly flexible structures and on actuation mechanisms for aerodynamic load control. Recent studies have included coupled aeroelastic and flight dynamics of low-speed high-altitude air vehicles, fatigue load reduction on wind turbine blades under atmospheric turbulence, and aerodynamic shape control on micro air vehicles.

Ruben Sanchez Fernandez

Ph.D. Candidate, Department of Aeronautics, Imperial College London

My research aims to investigate the behaviour of actuated membrane wings immersed in low-Reynolds number flows. The objective is to effectively address the complex interactions that arise from the flow-driven deformations of the membrane and the effects that they induce in the flow. My initial contribution to SU2 has been the improvement of the FEM structural solver. My future work will deal with the analysis of the Fluid-Structure Interaction problem of membranes made of complex materials using SU2.
